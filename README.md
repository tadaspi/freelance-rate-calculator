## The Problem

Most freelancers undercharge by 30-50%. Why? They set rates based on what feels right, not what the math says they need.

If you want **$80,000 take-home pay**, you need to earn **$120,000+** to cover:

| Expense | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| Self-employment tax (15.3%) | $12,240 |
| Federal + state income tax (~25%) | $20,000 |
| Health insurance | $8,000 |
| Business expenses | $5,000 |
| **Total needed** | **$125,240** |

And that's before accounting for non-billable hours (admin, marketing, invoicing) — typically 30-40% of your time.

## The Formula

```
Required Revenue = (Take-Home + Taxes + Expenses) / Billable Hours Ratio
Hourly Rate = Required Revenue / Billable Hours Per Year
```

## Example Calculation

**Goal:** $80,000 take-home pay

- Total revenue needed: $125,240
- Billable hours ratio: 60% (40% on admin/marketing)
- Work weeks: 48 (4 weeks vacation/sick)
- Hours per week: 40
- Billable hours: 48 × 40 × 0.60 = **1,152 hours**

**Minimum hourly rate: $109/hour**

Most freelancers charging $50/hr for this scenario are losing money.
